**💰 Customer Churn & Retention Analytics System**

(Power BI + SQL + Python)

**🚀 Project Overview**

This project analyzes customer churn behavior to understand why customers leave, which segments are at risk, and how much revenue is lost due to churn. It combines SQL-based data analysis, Python-driven exploration, and Power BI dashboards to deliver actionable retention insights.

**🎯 Business Problem**

Businesses lose significant revenue not only from low acquisition but from existing customers leaving (churn).

This project answers:
Which customers are leaving and why?
What patterns indicate churn risk?
How much revenue is being lost?
Which customers should be targeted for retention?


**📊 Key Objectives**
Measure churn rate and customer retention performance
Identify high-risk customer segments
Analyze revenue loss due to churn
Understand behavioral patterns behind churn
Build actionable retention insights for business decisions


**🛠️ Tech Stack**
SQL → KPI extraction, segmentation, churn metrics
Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn) → EDA & analysis
Power BI → Interactive dashboards & storytelling


**📂 Dataset**
IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset

Fields include: customer demographics, contract type, tenure, billing, services, churn status

📊 Power BI Dashboard Pages

🟢 1. Executive Overview
This section provides a high-level snapshot of overall customer health, summarizing churn performance and revenue impact in a single view. It helps stakeholders quickly understand business retention status and financial exposure due to customer attrition.

Key visuals include:
Total customer count
Churn rate indicator
Active vs churned customer breakdown
Revenue lost due to churn
Insight focus:
This view helps identify whether the business is retaining customers effectively and highlights the immediate financial impact of churn, enabling faster executive decision-making.

🟡 2. Customer Segmentation

This section analyzes how different customer groups behave and identifies which segments are most prone to churn. It helps uncover structural patterns in churn based on contract type, tenure, payment behavior, and service usage.

Key focus areas:

Churn by customer tenure (early vs long-term users)
Churn by contract type (month-to-month vs long-term contracts)
Churn by payment method (billing friction patterns)
Churn by internet/service usage type

Insight focus:
This view helps identify high-risk customer segments and reveals that churn is often driven by contract structure and early lifecycle behavior rather than random customer loss.

🔴 3. Risk Analysis

This section classifies customers into risk tiers to support proactive retention strategies and early intervention planning.

Key focus areas:

High-risk customer identification
Medium-risk customer grouping
Low-risk stable customers
Risk segmentation distribution

Insight focus:
This view enables targeted retention campaigns by prioritizing customers most likely to churn, improving efficiency of marketing and retention efforts.

🔵 4. Revenue Impact

This section quantifies the financial impact of churn by identifying revenue loss across customer segments and high-value customers.

Key focus areas:

Revenue loss by churned customers
High-value customer attrition analysis
Customer lifetime value (CLV) insights

Insight focus:
This view translates churn into financial terms, helping stakeholders understand how customer loss directly affects business revenue and profitability.

🟣 5. Behavioral Insights

This section explores behavioral patterns that drive churn over time and identifies operational or service-related causes behind customer attrition.

Key focus areas:

Churn trend over time
Customer usage behavior patterns
Service-level churn drivers

Insight focus:
This view helps uncover why customers leave by analyzing behavioral signals and service interactions, enabling data-driven improvements in customer experience.

📈 Key Insights (example style)
Customers with month-to-month contracts show significantly higher churn rates
Early-tenure customers are more likely to leave
Fiber optic users show higher churn compared to DSL users
Higher monthly charges correlate with increased churn probability
